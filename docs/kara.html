Project Kara

Kara is a conversational user interfaces that helps improve efficiency of group ordering, for a variety of different scenarios including home parties, company meetings, field trips, etc.

Kara looks for nearby restaurants that take group orders and provides you with a list of places for you to choose from. It allows you to put in your own preferences and search in specific scenarios, while putting in your special instructions to be sent to the restaurants. It also allows pre-ordering, so that you can plan ahead.

Ideation


After preliminary research, we decided to work on a CUI that takes group orders. We name our CUI Kara, since it sounds like a name of a friend and is very easy to pronounce. We then listed out some activities that we want Kara to support.

We also went through experience prototyping to gure out more user needs that we didn’t discover in our design process. We asked a few other groups to act in our scenario and recorded what questions they asked and what confusion they had. During the experience prototypring, some people were confused about the food options - “Does it only have pizza ordering, or does it have something else?“; some people raised allergy problems and asked Kara to check for ingredients of food; some people asked for suggestions, like “How should I order?“ or more speci c concerns like “I think there are too many chickens in the group meal.“

Iteration


To get prepared for the owchart of our nal design, we went through our re ned preattentives and attentives, and listed out the utterances and responses for each attentive using stickers. In the “order food“ attentive, we included multiple features of setting delivery time, recommending food types and restaurants, reminding of utensils, reminding of drinks, detecting change in location using GPS, etc. We also played around the CUI prototyping online tool API.AI (Diagflow) and built a simple version of Kara to test with.

After the two rounds of experience prototyping, critique, and refinements, we created the conversational flowchart of Kara, on both the general ordering procedure and the speci c user ow in the party ordering scenario we worked on. The ow charts consisted of preattentives and attentives, each has different utterances and responses, with a few error-catching features with response.
